About This Tool

The Chapel Hill Expert Survey (CHES) is the definitive academic dataset of European party positions, compiled since 1999 by political scientists who rate each party on dozens of policy dimensions. This explorer embeds the full 1999–2024 trend dataset and lets you navigate the ideological landscape of European politics interactively.

The two axes that explain the most variance in European party competition are lrgen (general left–right, 0 = far left, 10 = far right) and galtan (green/alternative/libertarian ↔ traditional/authoritarian/nationalist, 0 = fully libertarian, 10 = fully authoritarian). Together they define a two-dimensional ideological plane that maps onto almost every European coalition dynamic. EU position (0 = strongly opposed, 10 = strongly pro-EU) adds a third dimension that has reshaped party systems since 2010, particularly in Central and Eastern Europe.

Use the controls below to explore any combination of axes. Hover over dots to see full party details. The cluster section groups parties by positional similarity regardless of national boundaries — revealing ideological siblings that might never appear on the same ballot.
